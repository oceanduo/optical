Have Any Questions?

At what age should child see an optometrist?

It is recommended that children have their first comprehensive eye exam at the age of 3-5 years old. This is because early detection of any visual problems or eye diseases is essential for ensuring proper eye development and maintaining good vision. Even if a child appears to have good vision, an eye exam can help detect any underlying issues that may affect their vision in the future.

After the first eye exam, children should have their eyes examined every 1-2 years, or as recommended by their optometrist. Children who have a family history of eye problems, wear glasses, or have a learning disability may need to have more frequent eye exams.

Are eye tests and glasses covered by Medicare?

A comprehensive eye test can be bulk billed at ABC optical to all valid Medicare cardholders. This includes, but is not limited to, citizens and permanent residents of Australia and New Zealand, or residents of the Christmas, Norfolk and Cocos islands.

But government Medicare services will not cover the cost of glasses, contact lenses, corrective eye surgery or other optical procedures,most private health funds have an option for covering glasses as part of a specific policy. This type of coverage is called optical extras and they can be used at optical stores and enable no gap purchases on many optical appliances.

What happens during an eye test?

You need to first book an eye test with us. You can do this online or visit our store to make an appointment. Fill out your details correctly and make sure to bring your old glasses, medicare or health fund card if you have them. When you arrive, you will be checked in and asked for your medical history, family medical history, why you are here, what concerns you have, any symptoms and any existing prescriptions. Before you see your optometrist, you will complete pre-testing with one of our optical assistants. Once finished with these diagnostic tests, you will see an optometrist. The optometrist will conduct their own tests. When all the tests have been completed, your optometrist will discuss your results with you, and make recommendations for prescriptions or further treatment if needed. Our friendly staff will then take over to discuss lens options and help you choose a pair of glasses. Dispensing for new glasses often takes 2-3 weeks.
